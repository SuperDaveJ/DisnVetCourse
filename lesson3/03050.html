Paying Attention to Detail

  • Classic Disney films have an enduring popularity
  • Following Walt's model: an amazing level of detail
  • WALL-E delivers an amazingly rich, detailed wealth of information

Watch the video clip paying special attention to the huge amount of detail visible in Wall-E's home.

Narrator

Viewing classic Disney films, such as Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs (1937), Cinderella (1950), or Mary Poppins (1964), is the first Disney experience for many of our guests.

Those films, even though released years ago, are still popular. They stand up to repeated viewings, in part, because Walt Disney filled his movies with an amazing level of detail.

We, at Disney, still follow Walt's model. One example is the highly successful 2008 Disney/PIXAR release Wall-E.

Wall-E delivered a moving story. In the story, Wall-E is a lone robot left on earth after humans have abandoned the planet. His job is to clean it up.

Wall-E, following Walt Disney's approach, also delivered an amazingly rich, incredibly detailed, wealth of information.

In this video, Wall-E returns to his home at the end of a workday. Pay special attention to the huge amount of detail visible in Wall-E's home.
