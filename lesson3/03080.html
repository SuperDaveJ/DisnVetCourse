Narrator

Adding all those details is expensive. And, few will notice. So why do we do it? The big "wow" of a story well told is certainly important. But at Disney, we also believe in the little "wow."

The big "wow" is expected. The little "wow" is not. As a result, it connects with our guests in ways that surprise and delight them. As Andrew Stanton, the director/writer of WALL-E, explained, "it's the little whispers that speak to an audience."

It is in this myriad of small details that expectations are exceeded. People expect the product or service to work. When they experience an effective product AND additional surprising emotional connections, they become committed to the service provider.

This commitment draws guests back to Disney Parks and Resorts year after year. It is the essence of Disney's Quality Service definition in action.
