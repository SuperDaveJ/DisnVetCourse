Paying Attention to Detail

Viewing classic Disney films, such as Snow White and the Seven Dwarves (1937), Cinderella (1950), and Mary Poppins (1964) is the first Disney experience for many of our guests.

Those films, even though they were all released years ago, are still popular. They stand up to repeated viewings, in part, because Walt Disney filled his movies with an amazing level of detail.

We, at Disney, still follow Walt's model. One example is the highly successful 2008 Disney/PIXAR release WALL-E.

WALL-E delivered a moving story. In the story, WALL-E is a lone robot left on earth after humans have abandoned the planet. His job is to clean it up.

In keeping with Walt Disney's approach, WALL-E also delivered an amazingly rich, incredibly detailed, wealth of information.

Watch the video clip on this screen. In it, WALL-E returns to his home at the end of a workday. Pay special attention to the huge amount of detail visible in WALL-E's home.
