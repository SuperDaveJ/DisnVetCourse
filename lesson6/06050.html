Balancing Quality Standards: The Haunted Mansion

Prioritized Quality standards provide employees with the tools needed to make the right decisions and deliver consistent quality service.

Narrator 1

This example comes from The Haunted Mansion.

The Haunted Mansion is a ride-through tour of a haunted house where a friendly Ghost Host introduces you to his closest 999 dearly-departed friends.

Imagine an operations cast member working the load position on one of the busiest days of the year.

Working the load position, the cast member walks on a belt that moves at the same speed as the ride vehicles. The cast member notices an elderly guest using a walker, who is having a difficult time stepping onto the belt. The line begins backing up.

Given the situation, the cast member thinks the following:

Narrator 2

  • Is this a safety, courtesy, show or efficiency issue? In some ways, it's all four.
  • Efficiency suggests that getting guests on the ride quickly is important, and the guy is holding things up. I should tell the guy to get out of line, but …
  • Show would be undercut if I tell him that, and…
  • Courtesy would be undercut if I embarrass him in front of everyone.
  • Safety could be a big issue, if the guest cannot manage the belt.
  • Oh no, guest is still having trouble. Safety is most important. It takes precedence over Efficiency, Show and Courtesy. I'll stop the ride.

Narrator 1

For the guests already on the ride, the show has been interrupted. They might complain that the ride broke down, but safety comes first.

To maintain the show for them, an announcement will be made: "Prankish spirits have interrupted our journey. Please remain seated in your doom buggy."

The announcement does not say, "Slow guest at load."

The cast member has averted potential problems because he knew the priority of the Disney Quality Standards. In widely varying circumstances all day long, cast members will rely on the Disney Quality Standards to help them determine how to respond to unusual situations.
