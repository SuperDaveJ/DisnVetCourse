Emotions

A family arrived at the Park very early today in a rental car. It’s now the end of the day, there are thousands of  cars parked in the parking lot. They don’t remember where they parked their white rental car.

At that moment the Compass tells us the guests’ likely state of mind:

  • Need – go home
  • Want – find the car
  • Stereotype – no one can help
  • Emotion – frustration and hopelessness

A parking lot cast member stops his van and approaches the guests, asking, “The car moved on you, huh?” The family laughs. The cast member asks the family what time of day they arrived. The family recalls that they were so intent on an early arrival, they never paused to notice their parking location. The cast member then pulls out a clipboard, looks at it and says, “You are parked in Goofy rows 52-53. Hop in; I’ll take you there.

We intentionally track the time of day each row is parked so that we can serve our guests at their emotional level.  The family was tired and the parking incident threatened to be an unpleasant end to their day.  As if by magic, the cast member has the appropriate information, and the family’s emotion is one of relief that quickly turns to amazement. The addition of the van ride creates a special, unexpected, service “wow.”

The result? An extremely negative situation that would have been discussed for years has instead become an amazing story of Disney exceeding all service expectations.

It wasn’t magic. It was Guestology.
