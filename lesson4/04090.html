Narrator

There are, of course, stereotypes about Disney.

  • It's only for kids
  • It's too expensive
  • The lines are long
  • It's a happy place
  • It's so clean
  • Everybody is so friendly
  • It's safe

Some of these are positive stereotypes and some are negative. Each cast member has a responsibility to deliver on the positive stereotypes and overcome the negative.

For example, cleanliness is a positive stereotype. Disney has a third shift that works all night scrubbing the streets and keeping the grounds clean, but to ensure that the stereotype is maintained, every Disney cast member, even the CEO, is expected to pick up trash when they see it.

By maintaining positive stereotypes, we meet our guests' expectations. But negative stereotypes give us a real opportunity to exceed expectations. If you can turn a negative around, you create a "wow."

For instance, Disney is aggressively working to make lines seem shorter. Entertainers perform for guests in the queues, operations personnel play games with children in line, free Fast Passes give guests a specific time to return to an attraction for expedited boarding, and many queues are being redesigned with interactive elements that make the queues as engaging as the ride experience itself!

By enhancing your known positive stereotypes and working to minimize negative stereotypes, we work to exceed Veterans' expectations. What negative stereotypes about VA care can you try to minimize? What positive stereotypes can you enhance?
